Example A.3.2. Using partial fractions: Including a quadratic term.

We want to find an antiderivative of \(\frac{3x^2+4x+29}{(x-1)(x^2+4x+13)}\text{.}\) Since \(x^2+4x+13=(x+2)^2+9\text{,}\) the second factor in the denominator has no real roots. Using partial fractions, we here have to find real constants \(A, B\) and \(D\) such that
\begin{equation*} \frac{3x^2+4x+29}{(x-1)(x^2+4x+13)} = \frac{A}{x-1}+\frac{Bx+D}{x^2+4x+13}. \end{equation*}
To this end, we multply this equation with the denominator \(q(x)=(x-1)(x^2+4x+13)\) to obtain
\begin{align*} 3x^2+4x+29 & = A\, (x^2+4x+13)+(Bx+D)(x-1) \\ & = (A+B)\, x^2+(4\,A-B+D)\,x+(13\,A-D) \end{align*}
Either by comparing coefficients or by using appropriate values for \(x\text{,}\) this yields
\begin{equation*} A=2,\quad B=1,\quad\text{and}\quad D=-3. \end{equation*}
Thus, we have
\begin{align*} \frac{3x^2+4x+29}{(x-1)(x^2+4x+13)} & = \frac{2}{x-1}+\frac{x-3}{x^2+4x+13}\\ & = \frac{2}{x-1}+\frac{\frac12(2x+4)-5}{x^2+4x+13}\\ & = \frac{2}{x-1}+\frac12\cdot \frac{2x+4}{x^2+4x+13}+\frac{-5}{x^2+4x+13}\\ & = \frac{2}{x-1}+\frac12\cdot \frac{2x+4}{x^2+4x+13}-\frac{5}{(x+2)^2+9}\\ & = \frac{2}{x-1}+\frac12\cdot \frac{2x+4}{x^2+4x+13}-\frac{5}{9}\cdot \frac1{\left(\frac{x+2}3\right)^2+1}, \end{align*}
and we therefore find
\begin{gather*} \int \frac{3x^2+4x+29}{(x-1)(x^2+4x+13)}\operatorname{d}\!{x} \\ = 2\,\ln|{x-1}|+\frac12\,\ln|{x^2+4x+13}|-\frac59\cdot 3\,\arctan\left(\frac{x+2}3\right)+C \\ = 2\,\ln|{x-1}|+\frac12\,\ln|{x^2+4x+13}|-\frac53\, \arctan\left(\frac{x+2}3\right)+C\\ = \ln\left((x-1)^2\cdot\sqrt{x^2+4x+13}\right)-\frac53\, \arctan\left(\frac{x+2}3\right)+C. \end{gather*}
